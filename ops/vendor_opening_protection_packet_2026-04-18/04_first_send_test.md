# First-Send Test For The Opening Protection Packet

## Goal
Find out whether this packet is a real workflow tool or just a good-looking theory.

## Target user
One of:

- opening-protection contractor owner
- office manager
- intake coordinator
- estimator

## Exact moment to test
Use the packet only in this moment:

`homeowner says they already have the MSFH report -> before estimator scheduling`

## What to send
Send:

1. the public scope brief

Do not add extra materials during the first test. Keep estimator handoff, boundary language, and other internal tools behind the homeowner reply.

## What counts as first send
`first send = 1` only if:

- the vendor sends the scope brief to a real homeowner
- the homeowner opens it or replies with the requested report page, photos, or clarified scope

## What counts as useful
At least one of:

- the public brief is opened
- the brief link or send note is copied
- the homeowner replies with the requested report page, photos, or clarified scope
- the estimator says the job is easier to classify
- the homeowner returns more organized
- there is less back-and-forth before scheduling
- a broad request gets narrowed before quote time

## What is noise
Do not count internal route clicks, estimator-sheet navigation, quote-boundary browsing, or PDF-export opens as proof that the free layer is repeating.

## Weak signals
These are not enough by themselves:

- "looks good"
- "interesting idea"
- "send me more info"
- internal praise with no real homeowner send

## Kill conditions after 2 weeks
Treat the packet as failed if:

- 20 targeted vendors hear the idea and no one sends it
- vendors reply but do not use it in a real inquiry
- homeowners ignore it or still return with the same confusion
- estimators say it does not change classification speed

## Continue conditions
Keep going if:

- one vendor sends it twice
- two vendors each send it once
- at least one real inquiry comes back cleaner than before

## Next step only after continue
Only after repeated sends should this move toward:

- vendor-branded version
- county-specific tailoring
- intake form version
- paid setup discussion
