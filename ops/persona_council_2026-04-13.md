## Forced question
If a solo founder is choosing whether to productize a Florida lead-gen asset around MSFH, what is the sharpest version worth building?

## Demand realist
- Yes, with scope discipline.
- The hot user is not asking if the program exists.
- The hot user already touched the program and now has to make a real money decision:
  - which improvement first
  - which contractor type
  - what the grant really covers

## SERP realist
- Yes, but only in the post-inspection wedge.
- Exact brand FAQs are crowded by the program itself.
- There is still room where the official pages stop at procedure and the homeowner needs judgment.

## Program realist
- The product must respect these facts:
  - inspection and grant are separate
  - only recommended and later observed improvements qualify
  - the homeowner owns contractor selection risk
  - current operational rules live in the Support Center more than in old PDFs

## Funnel operator
- Yes.
- The funnel is clean if the CTA follows the recommendation:
  - opening protection -> window or shutter contractor path
  - roof-related recommendation -> roofing or retrofit path
  - contractor confusion -> quote checklist first
- No phone-heavy brokerage is required.

## Sponsor seller
- Yes.
- Windows, shutters, roofing, and retrofit work can all support meaningful lead values.
- The commercial trap is pretending every page should immediately sell a quote.

## Risk operator
- Yes, with heavy guardrails.
- This category attracts scammy messaging.
- The site must never:
  - promise approval
  - promise reimbursement
  - imply affiliation with the state
  - imply every roof or window project qualifies

## Content strategist
- Yes.
- The strongest content is not `What is MSFH?`
- The strongest content is:
  - `What does this recommendation mean?`
  - `What should I do first?`
  - `What will the program actually pay for?`
  - `What should I ask before I sign?`

## Technical architect
- Yes.
- This can be built cleanly with a file-backed route system and an improvement-decision matrix.
- No database is required for phase 1.

## Portfolio allocator
- Approves as a `priority-2` active candidate.
- It is strong enough to package now and potentially build soon.
- It does not beat buried oil tank on wedge purity, but it is still better than most broad home-service ideas.

## Judge
- Final decision: `buildable now, but as priority-2`
- Confidence: `8.2 / 10`

## The winning one-sentence product definition
Build a Florida-only post-inspection decision engine that helps MSFH homeowners interpret recommended improvements, choose the right project first, and safely route into the right contractor type.

## The biggest trap
Do not drift into `official FAQ mirror + contractor directory`. That loses both trust and SEO sharpness.
