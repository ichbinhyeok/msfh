# Wedge Focus - 2026-04-13

## The only wedge that matters right now
Do not start with:

- general eligibility content
- `is the program open` style news pages
- broad hurricane-hardening education
- generic Florida insurance content
- county pages with no measured demand

Start with:

- homeowner received an initial inspection report
- homeowner is trying to understand recommended improvements
- homeowner must decide which project to do first
- homeowner needs to compare quotes and choose the right contractor type

Support, but do not lead with:

- `group-5` frustration pages
- `RFI` and portal-status pages
- final inspection or draw-request troubleshooting

## Winning user state
The winning user is already in one of these states:

1. Report received and improvements are confusing
2. Grant path is open, but contractor selection is the blocker
3. Opening protection is recommended, but windows versus shutters is unclear
4. Roof-related recommendation exists, but the homeowner does not know whether full roof work is justified
5. The homeowner needs to avoid paying for work the program will not reimburse

## Pages that should win first
- `msfh inspection report what next`
- `which MSFH project should I do first`
- `MSFH contractor quote checklist`
- `opening protection under MSFH`
- `roof replacement under MSFH`

Only after that wedge starts working:

- `no recommended improvements`
- `group 5 cannot proceed`
- `RFI`
- `draw request` or `final inspection`

## Pages that should wait
- broad Florida insurance explainers
- generic impact window pages with no MSFH decision angle
- county overlays with no route evidence
- pure status pages that duplicate the Support Center

## Public-growth focus for the first 4 to 6 weeks
Primary CTA order:

1. `Get the contractor quote checklist for your recommended improvement`
2. `Find the right contractor type for this project`
3. `Compare likely project paths before you commit`

Secondary CTA:

- `See what the program will and will not reimburse`
- `Understand the safest next step before contractor confirmation`

## Narrow operating loop
For the first implementation cycle, every build decision should support one loop:

1. User lands on a statewide program or improvement route
2. User identifies their scenario:
  - report received
  - opening protection
  - roof-related
  - contractor choice
3. Page clarifies what the recommendation really means
4. Page explains what the program will and will not cover
5. Page routes the user to the correct lead form or checklist

## First traction signal
The first real traction signal is not traffic.

It is:

- repeated clicks on `quote checklist` and `find contractor type` CTAs from program and improvement pages
- followed by at least a few qualified lead submissions tied to specific improvement categories

## Operator memo
Do not try to beat the official FAQ on being official. Win the moment when the homeowner has the report, feels uncertain, and still has to choose what to do next.
