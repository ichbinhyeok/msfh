# Source Audit - 2026-04-13

## Source hierarchy
For this product, source trust order matters because the program changes operationally.

Use this order:

1. current `mysafeflhome.com` pages
2. current MSFH Support Center operational articles
3. official Florida Senate or DFS/CFO program updates
4. older homeowner guide PDFs for background only
5. secondary contractor or media pages only for SERP and commercial pattern checks

## Official anchors

### Current program homepage
- URL: `https://mysafeflhome.com/`
- Why it matters:
  - confirms the program is currently presented as active
  - shows current public workflow:
    - create account
    - complete prioritization questionnaire
    - inspection group
    - inspection application
    - free wind-mitigation inspection
    - review report
    - apply for grant if eligible
  - emphasizes the report as a roadmap to retrofitting and insurance savings
- Product consequence:
  - the product can credibly orient around the report stage, not only pre-inspection awareness

### FAQ page
- URL: `https://mysafeflhome.com/faq/`
- Why it matters:
  - says inspection and grant are separate applications
  - repeats contractor-liability disclaimer
- Product consequence:
  - do not blur inspection and grant
  - do not imply the product is the program or that it controls contractor quality

### Support Center hub
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/en/support/solutions/156000560066`
- Why it matters:
  - current operational truth source for statuses, inspections, grants, project completion, and disbursement
- Product consequence:
  - route design should reflect the structure of real homeowner confusion after the report, not generic head terms

### What is the program?
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000024195-what-is-the-my-safe-fl-home-program-`
- Why it matters:
  - defines the two main components
  - confirms matching grants and low-income grants
  - states the program is not responsible for contractor selection or performance
- Product consequence:
  - lead-routing value is real because the program leaves contractor selection to the homeowner

### Eligibility for inspection
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000024190-who-is-eligible-to-receive-a-hurricane-mitigation-inspection-`
- Why it matters:
  - current inspection eligibility:
    - single-family detached or townhouse
    - site-built
    - owner-occupied
    - homestead exemption
- Product consequence:
  - avoid spending launch surface on condos, rentals, mobile homes, or generic `can I qualify` content

### Eligibility for grant
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/en/support/solutions/articles/156000024911-who-is-eligible-for-a-hurricane-mitigation-grant-`
- Why it matters:
  - current grant requirements include:
    - inspection through the program
    - insured value cap
    - pre-2008 permit date
    - contractor name and license number
    - mitigation project based on recommended improvements
    - final inspection
    - grant application within `24 months` of the initial inspection
  - explicitly says work done before grant approval will be denied
- Product consequence:
  - project-planning pages must warn against premature contracts or premature work

### Initial inspection report timing and contents
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/en/support/solutions/articles/156000367651-where-do-i-find-the-results-from-my-initial-inspection-report-`
- Why it matters:
  - report should appear within `14 days`
  - report includes:
    - recommended improvements
    - improvement cost estimates
    - 1802 summary
- Product consequence:
  - report interpretation is a credible standalone route family

### Recommended improvements
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/en/support/solutions/articles/156000367652-where-do-i-find-my-recommended-improvements-on-the-initial-inspection-report-`
- Why it matters:
  - recommended improvements vary by home
  - opening protection requires careful reading because not every opening may qualify
- Product consequence:
  - opening-protection decision content is a real wedge

### No recommended improvements
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000279688-what-if-there-are-no-recommended-improvements-on-my-initial-inspection-report-`
- Why it matters:
  - if there are no recommended improvements, the homeowner cannot proceed with the grant application
- Product consequence:
  - this is a real confusion route, but it is not a phase-1 revenue wedge

### Reviewing and selecting eligible mitigation projects
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/en/support/solutions/articles/156000279740-where-do-i-review-and-select-my-eligible-mitigation-projects-in-the-applicant-portal-`
- Why it matters:
  - only recommended and later observed improvements are eligible
  - attached single-family homes are treated as townhouses
  - townhouses are only eligible for opening protection funding
- Product consequence:
  - attached-home logic must not be mixed carelessly with detached-home improvement pages

### Authorized improvements
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000024215-what-improvements-are-authorized-for-mitigation-projects-`
- Why it matters:
  - current improvement set is framed as:
    - opening protection
    - roof-to-wall attachment
    - roof deck attachment
    - secondary water resistance
  - no other construction is eligible
- Product consequence:
  - the site should not over-expand into other roof or insurance topics

### Maximum grant amount
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000024199-what-is-the-maximum-amount-i-can-receive-through-my-safe-fl-home-`
- Why it matters:
  - matching grant is `2-to-1` up to `$10,000`
  - low-income grants may provide up to `$10,000` without homeowner match
- Product consequence:
  - budget framing and project-priority calculators can be useful later

### Contractor selection
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000024208-how-do-i-choose-a-licensed-contractor-`
- Why it matters:
  - contractor must be licensed in eligible Florida categories
  - the program no longer provides a list of authorized program contractors
- Product consequence:
  - contractor-selection content is not optional; it is part of the product moat

### Contractor update flyer
- URL: `https://mysafeflhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/Working-With-a-Contractor-Flyer_2-Pager_6_29_24.pdf`
- Why it matters:
  - as of `2024-07-01`, the old authorized contractor program ended
  - homeowners choose and manage their own contractor
  - the flyer recommends multiple quotes, documentation, and license checks
- Product consequence:
  - quote checklist and contractor comparison pages are directly justified

### Building permits
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000024216-who-is-responsible-for-obtaining-building-permits-`
- Why it matters:
  - homeowners must ensure permits are obtained and closed before final inspection
- Product consequence:
  - quote pages should remind users that contractor management and permit closeout still matter

### Roof replacement
- URL: `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/en/support/solutions/articles/156000372425-can-i-replace-my-roof-`
- Why it matters:
  - roof covering replacement may be included when needed to complete recommended improvements
  - SWR logic matters
- Product consequence:
  - `roof replacement under MSFH` is a real high-intent route, but it must stay tightly tied to recommended work

### Portal terms and prioritization
- URLs:
  - `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000257052-applicant-portal-terms`
  - `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/en/support/solutions/articles/156000373892-msfh-2-0-applicant-portal-terms`
  - `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/support/solutions/articles/156000312846/`
  - `https://support.mysafeflhome.com/es/support/discussions/topics/156000651392`

  - current 1.0 vs 2.0 flow split exists
  - prioritization groups affect access timing

- Product consequence:
  - these are support and problem-state routes, not the primary launch wedge

## Secondary SERP signals

### Contractor marketing pages are already trying to capture this demand
- Example results seen in current SERPs:
  - roof pages
  - impact window pages
  - `approved contractor` style claims
- Product consequence:
  - a new domain should not try to win on raw contractor landing pages
  - the better angle is decision support after the report, before the homeowner commits

## Important source nuance
The `2023` Homeowner's Guide PDF is useful, but it includes older operational assumptions such as the old approved-contractor framing.

That means:

- do not treat the old PDF as the final word on live process details
- use current Support Center articles for current operations
- use the PDF mainly for concept framing, background, and report structure context

## Product consequence summary
- build around current post-report ambiguity
- make improvement-selection and quote preparation the center
- avoid exact-FAQ duplication unless the page adds a real decision layer
