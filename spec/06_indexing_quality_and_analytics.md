# 06 Indexing Quality And Analytics

## Indexing philosophy
Index only pages that are:

- post-inspection specific
- current-rule aware
- source-backed
- commercially meaningful or strategically clarifying

## Promotion recommendation principle
Held routes should not auto-promote just because they exist in the system.

The correct loop is:

1. metrics arrive
2. the agent reviews route evidence
3. the agent writes a recommendation
4. the user approves or rejects the promotion

This protects the wedge while removing the need for the human to remember every project.

## Pages that should index first
- home
- inspection-report page
- choose-project page
- contractor-quote page
- what-the-program-pays-for page
- five improvement pages
- strongest evergreen guides

## Pages that should stay `noindex` at first
- thin county overlays
- pure status pages
- partner finder pages
- deep draw-request help pages

## Route promotion rules
A route can move from `noindex` to `index` only when:

1. official source support is clear
2. the route materially differs from official procedural pages
3. the page has a realistic CTA or strategic role
4. the route has at least one strong internal-link source

## Event taxonomy
Track at minimum:

- `page_view`
- `route_cta_click`
- `improvement_select`
- `lead_form_open`
- `lead_submit_attempt`
- `lead_submit_success`
- `lead_submit_error`
- `partner_outbound_click`

## Early dashboard metrics
- indexed pages
- impressions by route family
- CTR by route family
- CTA clicks by route family
- lead submissions by improvement type
- lead submissions by county

## Promotion evidence inputs
Every promotion review should look at:

- search impressions and clicks by indexed parent route
- query evidence pointing to a held route need
- CTA clicks by improvement type
- lead submissions by improvement type
- source freshness
- partner availability for the route's commercial action

## First proof-of-traction metrics
- impressions on inspection-report and improvement pages
- clicks on quote-checklist or contractor-type CTAs
- lead submissions from opening-protection or roof-related pages

## Held-route recommendation rules

### No-recommended-improvements route
Recommend only when:

- the indexed report page shows repeated no-recommendations confusion
- the page can give a real next step and not just a dead-end answer

### Group-5 or prioritization routes
Recommend only when:

- contact notes or search evidence show repeated demand
- the page adds corrective or strategic value beyond a portal-status definition

### RFI or portal-status routes
Recommend only when:

- the site is already seeing meaningful operational-stage traffic
- the page can help users fix or avoid a common mistake

### County overlays
Recommend only when:

- statewide routes already prove demand
- county differences materially change the answer or partner density
- the page can stay lean and maintained

## Agent review cadence
- every meaningful implementation or review session after metrics exist
- every `14` days after launch, even if no route is promoted

If no route qualifies, the agent should still record `hold` with a reason so the user does not have to remember what was checked.

## Quality gates
- no page should be indexed without source freshness dates
- no route family should expand before the first route in that family earns impressions
- if a route has no meaningful decision value beyond the Support Center, keep it out of the index

## Kill rules
Kill or demote any route family that:

- attracts traffic but no action
- duplicates official support content without new value
- depends mostly on stale or secondary sources
- drifts into broad branded curiosity

## Analytics principle
The point of analytics is to learn which post-report decision states convert, not to admire branded pageviews.
